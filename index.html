About Me

I am a first-year PhD student in Computer Science at the University of Maryland. My research, guided by my advisors Leo Lampropoulos and Ian Miers, explores Programming Languages and Cryptography, with a focus on using formal methods and zero-knowledge proofs to verify private systems without exposing sensitive details.

As an undergrad, I worked on Automata Theory, extending domain-specific languages with tools to better understand nondeterminism.

I aim for my work to be driven by real-world application.
